Dutch Rental FAQ

Rental Rights & Obligations

Dutch law provides tenants with strong protections including:

  • Right to habitable living conditions
  • Protection against arbitrary eviction
  • Maximum rent increases based on inflation
  • Privacy and quiet enjoyment of property
  • Proper notice for landlord visits

No, landlords must provide reasonable notice (usually 24-48 hours) except in emergencies. You have the right to privacy and peaceful enjoyment of your rental property.

Deposit Return Process

Deposits must be returned within 14-30 days after the final inspection, provided:

  • No outstanding rent payments
  • Property is returned in good condition (normal wear and tear accepted)
  • All utility bills are settled
  • Keys are returned

Landlords can deduct for:

  • Unpaid rent or utilities
  • Damage beyond normal wear and tear
  • Professional cleaning if property is left dirty
  • Repair costs for damages caused by tenant

Deductions must be justified with receipts and evidence.

Maintenance Responsibilities

Landlords are responsible for:

  • Structural repairs (roof, walls, foundations)
  • Plumbing and electrical systems
  • Heating and hot water systems
  • Common areas in apartment buildings
  • Exterior maintenance

Tenants are typically responsible for:

  • Minor maintenance (light bulbs, battery replacement)
  • Keeping the property clean and hygienic
  • Preventing damage through negligence
  • Reporting issues promptly to prevent further damage
  • Garden maintenance (if specified in contract)

Notice Periods & Moving Out

For indefinite contracts, the notice period is typically:

  • Tenant: 1 calendar month
  • Landlord: 3 calendar months (with valid reason)

For fixed-term contracts, notice is usually not required as the contract ends automatically.

Before moving out, ensure you:

  • Give proper written notice
  • Schedule a final inspection with the landlord
  • Clean the property thoroughly
  • Repair any damages you caused
  • Settle all utility bills
  • Return all keys and access devices
  • Provide forwarding address for deposit return

Rent Increase Regulations

For 2024, the maximum rent increase for:

  • Social housing: 5.8% maximum
  • Private sector: No fixed maximum, but must be reasonable

Landlords can only increase rent once per year and must provide written notice at least 2 months in advance.

Yes, you can challenge a rent increase if:

  • It exceeds the maximum allowed percentage
  • Proper notice wasn't given
  • It's not the annual increase period
  • The increase is unreasonably high for the private sector

You can file a complaint with the Rent Tribunal (Huurcommissie) within 6 weeks of receiving notice.
