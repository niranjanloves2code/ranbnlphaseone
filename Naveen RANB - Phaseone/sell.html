Frequently Asked Questions

What is transfer tax and who pays it?

Transfer tax (Overdrachtsbelasting) is 2% of the purchase price for investment properties and second homes. Owner-occupiers are exempt from this tax when selling their primary residence.

Are estate agent fees negotiable?

Yes, fees are typically between 1-2% depending on services included. Full service at 2% includes professional photography, staging, viewings, and negotiation.

When do I need an energy label?

An energy label is mandatory when selling any property in the Netherlands. Costs range from €200-€400 depending on property size and complexity.

What are the notary costs for?

Notary costs cover the transfer deed preparation, registration at the Kadaster (land registry), and handling the financial settlement between parties.

Dutch Property Selling FAQ

Quick answers to common questions about selling in the Netherlands

The WOZ value (Waardering Onroerende Zaken) is the official government valuation of your property determined by your municipality. It's important for several reasons:

📊 Impact on Selling
  • • Buyers often compare asking price to WOZ value
  • • Affects property tax calculations
  • • Used by banks for mortgage assessments
⚖️ Appeals Process
  • • You can object within 6 weeks of valuation
  • • Need comparable property evidence
  • • Professional valuation can help your case
💡 Pro Tip

A WOZ value that's significantly lower than market value can be appealing to buyers, as it suggests lower property taxes.

Since 2015, an energy label is mandatory when selling any property in the Netherlands. Here's what you need to know:

Energy Label Description Impact on Value
A++ to B Highly energy efficient +3-8% value increase
C to D Average efficiency Neutral impact
E to G Low efficiency -2-5% value decrease
💰 Costs
  • Standard assessment: €200-€400
  • Larger properties: €400-€600
  • Processing time: 2-10 days
📝 Requirements
  • Must be provided before sale
  • Valid for 10 years
  • Registered in national database

Dutch law requires sellers to disclose specific information about their property. Failure to do so can lead to legal consequences.

Mandatory Disclosures

You must disclose known defects and issues that affect property value or livability.

🏠 Structural Issues
  • • Foundation problems
  • • Roof leaks or damage
  • • Moisture or mold issues
  • • Pest infestations
📋 Legal Matters
  • • Building permit violations
  • • Restrictive covenants
  • • Ongoing disputes with neighbors
  • • Service charge arrears (VvE)
🛡️ Protection

Our standard sales contract includes a comprehensive disclosure questionnaire to ensure you meet all legal obligations.

Transfer tax (Overdrachtsbelasting) is an important consideration in Dutch property transactions. Here's how it works:

2%
Standard Transfer Tax Rate
✅ Exemptions
  • Primary residence - Owner-occupiers pay 0%
  • First-time buyers - Under 35, €440,000 limit
  • Nature conservation - Specific protected properties
⚠️ Who Pays
  • Investors - Always pay 2%
  • Second homes - 2% applies
  • Commercial properties - Different rates apply
💡 Important Note

Transfer tax is typically paid by the buyer, but as a seller, understanding these rules helps you better advise potential buyers about total costs.

The notary plays a crucial role in Dutch property transactions, ensuring legal transfer of ownership.

1
Initial Agreement

Purchase agreement signed (2-4 weeks after offer)

2
Due Diligence

Notary verifies documents and contracts (2-3 weeks)

3
Mortgage Approval

Buyer's mortgage finalized (4-6 weeks)

4
Transfer Deed

Official signing at notary office (6-8 weeks)

5
Registration

Property registered at Kadaster (1-2 days)

📋 Notary Responsibilities
  • • Prepare transfer deed
  • • Verify identities
  • • Handle financial settlement
  • • Register at land registry
  • • Calculate transfer taxes
💰 Typical Costs
  • • Basic transfer: €1,500-€2,500
  • • Mortgage deed: Additional €500-€1,000
  • • Complex properties: €2,500-€4,000
  • • Kadaster fees: €50-€150
